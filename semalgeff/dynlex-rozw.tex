We are to consider extensions to handlers for a language with lexically-scoped handlers implemented with capabilities.
\begin{align*}
	&h ∷= \texttt{effect}\;x, r ⇒ e \\
	&|\texttt{effect}\;x, r ⇒ e|^\ell = λx.\,\textsf{shift}_0^\ell\,r.\,e
\end{align*}
We can borrow ideas for restricted forms of operations from Koka. There,
the above general form is marked with \texttt{ctl}.

\section{Values}
\begin{align*}
	&h ::= ... │ \texttt{val}\;v \\
	&|\texttt{val}\;v|^\ell = v
\end{align*}
This is useful for providing implicit values without the overhead of delimited control.
$$\texttt{handle `ask in enquirer () with val (fn () => 42)}$$

\section{Tail-resumptive operations}
\begin{align*}
	&h ::= ... │ \texttt{fun}\;x ⇒ e \\
	&|\texttt{fun}\;x ⇒ e|^\ell = λx.\,\textsf{shift}_0^\ell\,r.\,r\,e
\end{align*}
This common pattern may deserve its own syntax and can be understood as a
function executed in the handler's context. It also has the potential for a more efficient implementation.
$$\texttt{handle `ask in enquirer () with fun () => pick (0,1)}$$
